Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into small pieces
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 egg
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Curry leaves for garnish
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp red chili sauce (optional)
Preparation Time Cooking Time Difficulty Level
15-20 minutes 20-25 minutes Medium

Recipe

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, cornflour, all-purpose flour, and egg. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour.
  2. Heat oil: In a deep frying pan, heat enough oil to fry the chicken.
  3. Fry the chicken: Once the oil is hot, fry the marinated chicken pieces in batches until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Prepare the tempering: In another pan, heat a little oil. Add slit green chilies and curry leaves, saut√©ing for a few seconds until aromatic.
  5. Toss the chicken: Add the fried chicken to the tempering and toss everything together. Optionally, you can add red chili sauce for an extra kick.
  6. Finish and serve: Drizzle lemon juice over the chicken, give it a quick toss, and serve hot as an appetizer or side dish. Garnish with extra curry leaves if desired.

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton, cut into small pieces
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking
  • Coriander leaves for garnish
Preparation Time Cooking Time Difficulty Level
15-20 minutes 45-50 minutes Medium

Recipe

  1. Marinate the mutton: In a bowl, mix mutton with ginger-garlic paste, black pepper powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and salt. Let it marinate for 1-2 hours.
  2. Pressure cook the mutton: In a pressure cooker, add marinated mutton, water, and cook for about 20-25 minutes (or until tender). Set aside.
  3. Heat oil: In a pan, heat oil and fry chopped onions, green chilies, and curry leaves until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Cook tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  5. Prepare the masala: Add garam masala and the cooked mutton (along with any remaining stock). Stir well and cook until the mutton absorbs all the masala and becomes dry.
  6. Finish and serve: Garnish with coriander leaves and drizzle with lemon juice. Serve hot as a starter or with rice and roti.
